How to Count Characters for Every Social Media Platform

Every platform counts differently, and going one character over can truncate your message or block your post. Here are the current limits and how to stay inside them.

Why Character Limits Matter

Social platforms cap post length to keep feeds scannable, but the limits vary widely and some count emoji, links, and line breaks in surprising ways. Writing right up to the limit often backfires — the most engaging posts usually sit well below the maximum, leaving room for the message to breathe.

Current Character Limits (2026)

  • X (Twitter): 280 characters for standard accounts. URLs count as a fixed 23 characters, and some emoji count as two.
  • Instagram captions: up to 2,200 characters, but only the first ~125 show before "more." Bios are capped at 150.
  • LinkedIn: posts allow about 3,000 characters; the headline field is 220.
  • Facebook: posts technically allow over 60,000 characters, but engagement drops sharply past ~80.
  • SMS: a single message is 160 characters; emoji can drop that to 70.

The Engagement Sweet Spot

Data across platforms consistently shows that shorter posts outperform longer ones for reach and interaction. On X, posts around 71–100 characters tend to earn the most engagement. On Instagram, front-loading your key message in the first 125 characters matters because that is all most users see without tapping "more."

How Emoji and Links Affect Counts

Emoji are often encoded as two characters because of how Unicode represents them, so an emoji-heavy post can hit a limit faster than it looks. On X, every link counts as exactly 23 characters no matter its real length, thanks to automatic link shortening.

Key Takeaways

  • Know the limit for each platform before you write — they differ a lot.
  • Emoji can count as two characters; links on X count as 23.
  • Shorter posts usually outperform maxed-out ones.
  • Always count before posting to avoid truncation.
