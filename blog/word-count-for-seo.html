Ideal Word Count for SEO: What Google Actually Wants in 2026

Short answer: there is no magic number — but there are clear patterns. Heres what the data shows and how to apply it.

Does Word Count Affect Rankings?

Word count is not a direct ranking factor, but it correlates strongly with ranking performance. Longer content tends to cover more subtopics, earn more backlinks, and answer more related questions — all of which Google rewards. The key insight for 2026 is that intent determines the appropriate length, not the other way around.

Recommended Lengths by Content Type

  • Informational guides: 1,500–2,500 words
  • Commercial/comparison posts: 1,200–3,000 words
  • Transactional pages: 300–1,000 words
  • News/updates: 500–800 words

The Golden Rule

Write as long as necessary to fully answer the user query — then stop. Padding hurts rankings because it increases bounce rate. Trimming a bloated 2,800-word post down to a tight 1,600 words has been shown to improve rankings when the cut content was repetitive.

How to Check Your Word Count

Use our free Word Counter tool to instantly see your word count, keyword density, and reading time as you write. Aim to match or slightly exceed the average length of the top-ranking pages for your target keyword.

Key Takeaways

  • No fixed word count guarantees rankings.
  • Match length to search intent.
  • 1,500–2,500 words works for most competitive blog posts.
  • Quality and completeness beat raw length every time.
