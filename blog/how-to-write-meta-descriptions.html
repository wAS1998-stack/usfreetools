How to Write Meta Descriptions That Get Clicks

Your meta description is the free ad copy under every search result. Writing it well can lift click-through rate significantly — here is how.

What a Meta Description Does

The meta description is the short summary that appears beneath your page title in search results. It is not a direct ranking factor, but it heavily influences whether someone clicks — and click-through rate does affect performance over time. Think of it as the most-read sentence you will ever write about your page.

The Ideal Length

Aim for 150–160 characters. Google truncates descriptions around there on desktop and shorter on mobile (often around 120). Front-load your most important words so they survive any truncation. Check your length precisely with our meta description counter as you write.

A Simple Formula

  • Lead with the benefit or answer the searcher is looking for.
  • Include your primary keyword naturally — Google bolds matching terms.
  • Add a reason to click: free, fast, step-by-step, no signup, updated for 2026.
  • End with a soft call to action where it fits.

Common Mistakes

  • Duplicating descriptions across pages — each should be unique.
  • Keyword stuffing — it reads as spam and Google may rewrite it.
  • Being too vague — generic descriptions waste the click opportunity.
  • Ignoring intent — match what the searcher actually wants.

Will Google Use My Description?

Not always. Google sometimes generates its own snippet from on-page text when it judges that more relevant to a specific query. Writing a strong description still matters because it is used most of the time and sets the tone you want.

Key Takeaways

  • Keep descriptions to 150–160 characters and front-load key words.
  • Make every description unique and intent-matched.
  • Include a clear reason to click.
  • Avoid stuffing — write for humans first.
