The Units and How They Relate

The metric units stack neatly by tens: 1,000 grams make a kilogram, and 1,000 kilograms make a metric tonne. The imperial side is less tidy: 16 ounces make a pound, and 14 pounds make a stone — a unit still widely used for body weight in the UK and Ireland. The bridge between the systems is that one kilogram is about 2.205 pounds, and one ounce is about 28.35 grams.

Because the imperial units do not follow a simple base-ten pattern, converting between them by hand is genuinely fiddly, and mixing systems — say, a recipe that lists some ingredients in grams and others in ounces — is exactly where mistakes happen. A converter that holds all the units at once lets you move between any of them without juggling multiple factors, so you can work confidently across both systems.

Where a Multi-Unit Weight Converter Helps

Cooking and baking are perhaps the most common use, because recipes travel across borders and measurement systems freely. A recipe from Europe lists ingredients in grams, an American one in ounces and pounds, and a British one might use both. Baking in particular rewards precision — too much flour by weight genuinely changes the result — so accurately converting between grams and ounces matters for how a dish turns out. Having every unit in one converter means you can follow any recipe without hunting for the right conversion.

Beyond the kitchen, weight conversion is everyday practical. Shipping and travel involve weight limits quoted in kilograms or pounds depending on the carrier, and going over can mean extra fees. Product specifications, postal rates, fitness and body-weight tracking, and countless DIY and craft projects all involve weights that may need translating between systems. Because this tool covers grams, kilograms, ounces, pounds, and stone together, it handles whatever combination you encounter without forcing you to chain several conversions.

Quick Tips

  • One kilogram is about 2.205 pounds, and one ounce is about 28.35 grams — the key bridges between systems.
  • Sixteen ounces make a pound; fourteen pounds make a stone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many grams are in an ounce?

One ounce is about 28.35 grams. So a quarter-pound, which is four ounces, is roughly 113 grams. This converter handles the exact figure for any value.

How many pounds are in a kilogram?

One kilogram is about 2.205 pounds. To go the other way, one pound is about 0.454 kilograms. The converter switches between them instantly.

What is a stone in weight?

A stone is 14 pounds, a unit still commonly used for body weight in the UK and Ireland. So 10 stone is 140 pounds, or about 63.5 kilograms.

Why convert by weight for baking?

Because baking is sensitive to precise quantities — measuring flour by weight rather than volume is far more accurate, and small differences change the result. Converting weights precisely helps your baking turn out as intended.
