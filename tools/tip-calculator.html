🍽️ Tip Calculator

Calculate the right tip and split the total bill evenly among any number of people. Choose your tip percentage and see per-person amounts instantly.

What This Tip Calculator Does

This calculator works out the tip on a bill and, if you are dining as a group, splits the total fairly between everyone. Enter the bill amount, choose a tip percentage, and tell it how many people are sharing — it returns the tip, the grand total, and the amount each person owes. It removes the awkward mental math at the end of a meal and the risk of someone under- or over-paying.

The detail that trips groups up is when to split. The fair approach is to add the tip first, then divide the total — so everyone shares the tip proportionally. Splitting the pre-tip bill and adding tips separately almost always leaves the math slightly off, which this calculator avoids.

How Tipping Math Actually Works

A tip is simply a percentage of the bill. A 20% tip on a $50 meal is $10, found by multiplying $50 by 0.20. The grand total is the bill plus the tip — $60 in that example. To split among friends, you divide that $60 grand total by the number of people, not the original $50.

A quick mental shortcut for a 20% tip: take 10% of the bill (just move the decimal one place left) and double it. For $50, that is $5 doubled to $10. For 15%, take that 10% figure and add half of it again. These tricks are handy when you want a rough number, but the calculator gives you the exact figure including the per-person split.

Tipping in the Real World

Tipping norms vary by situation, and knowing the rough expectations helps you tip appropriately without overthinking it. In the United States, sit-down restaurant service commonly falls in the 15% to 20% range, with 20% widely treated as the standard for good service. Other services — delivery, taxis, salons — have their own conventions. The calculator lets you set any percentage, so you can match whatever is appropriate for the situation in front of you.

For groups, a frequent point of confusion is the tax. Some people tip on the pre-tax amount and some on the post-tax total; both are accepted, with pre-tax being the traditional approach. Whatever you choose, the important thing for fairness is to calculate the tip and add it before dividing the bill, so that everyone contributes their share of both the meal and the gratuity.

Splitting a Bill Without Friction

Group dinners are where tip math causes the most stress, usually because people try to do it piecemeal. The clean method is simple: total the bill, add the tip to get the grand total, then divide by the number of people. Everyone pays an equal share that already includes their portion of the tip. This calculator does exactly that in one step.

If your group wants to split unevenly — because some ordered far more than others — the fairest compromise is to still calculate the tip as a percentage of the whole bill, then have each person cover their own items plus their proportional share of the tip. For an even split, though, the calculator's per-person figure is all you need to settle up cleanly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a tip?

Multiply the bill by the tip percentage as a decimal. A 20% tip on $50 is $50 × 0.20 = $10. Add it to the bill for the total. The calculator does this and also splits the total across your group.

Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax amount?

Both are accepted. Tipping on the pre-tax amount is the traditional standard, while tipping on the post-tax total is slightly more generous. Choose whichever you are comfortable with and apply it consistently.

How do I split a bill with a tip fairly?

Add the tip to the bill first, then divide the grand total by the number of people. This ensures everyone shares the tip proportionally rather than someone paying more or less of it.

What is a standard tip percentage?

For sit-down restaurant service in the US, 15% to 20% is common, with 20% widely considered standard for good service. Other services have their own norms, so adjust the percentage to fit the situation.

Quick Tips for Tipping

  • For a fast 20% tip, take 10% of the bill and double it — a reliable mental shortcut.
  • Always add the tip before splitting a group bill, so everyone shares it proportionally.
  • Pre-tax tipping is the traditional standard, but post-tax is perfectly acceptable.
  • For exceptional service, there is nothing wrong with going above the usual range.
