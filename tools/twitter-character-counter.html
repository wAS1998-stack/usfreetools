🐦 Twitter Character Counter

Count characters in real time to stay within the 280-character limit for posts on X (Twitter).

What This Tool Does

This tool counts characters specifically against the limit for posts on X (formerly Twitter), showing how much room you have left as you write. Type or paste your post and it tracks your character count against the limit, warning you as you approach it. It takes the guesswork out of fitting your thoughts into the available space, so your post is not cut off or rejected for being too long.

Writing within a strict character limit is a particular skill, and knowing exactly where you stand as you write makes it far easier. Rather than writing a post, finding it is too long, and hunting for words to cut, you can see your count in real time and shape the message to fit from the start. This counter is tuned for the platform's limit specifically.

How the Character Limit Works

Standard posts on X have a 280-character limit, counting letters, spaces, punctuation, and most symbols. There are some particulars worth knowing: links are counted as a fixed length regardless of how long the actual URL is, because the platform shortens them automatically. Some characters, including many emoji and characters from certain languages, can count as more than one character due to how text is encoded, so an emoji-heavy post reaches the limit faster than its visible length suggests.

This is exactly why an accurate, platform-aware counter helps. Eyeballing whether a post fits is unreliable, especially once links and emoji are involved, and discovering you are over the limit only when you try to post is frustrating. Seeing the real count as you write lets you craft the post to fit precisely — trimming where needed, knowing how much room a link will really take, and being aware when emoji are eating into your space. It turns staying within the limit from a guessing game into something you can see and control.

Writing Effective Posts Within the Limit

The character limit is a constraint, but a productive one — it pushes you toward clarity and concision. Within the available space, the strongest posts lead with their most important point, since the opening is what shows in feeds and grabs attention, and because front-loading the message means it lands even if someone reads only the start. Cutting filler words, choosing precise language, and making every character earn its place are the skills the limit rewards, and watching your count as you write helps you practice them.

There are practical tactics too. If a thought genuinely needs more than the limit allows, breaking it into a connected series of posts is the accepted approach, with each post working within the limit while building on the last. Being mindful of how links and emoji consume space helps you budget your characters — knowing a link takes a fixed chunk lets you plan the surrounding text. And leaving a little room rather than maxing out every character can make a post easier to quote or respond to. The counter supports all of this by keeping the real, platform-accurate count in front of you, so you can focus on writing well within the space rather than worrying whether it fits.

  • Lead with your most important point, since the opening shows in feeds and grabs attention.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the character limit for a post on X?

Standard posts have a 280-character limit, counting letters, spaces, punctuation, and most symbols. This counter tracks your text against that limit as you write.

How are links counted?

Links count as a fixed length regardless of how long the actual URL is, because the platform automatically shortens them. So a long link does not consume proportionally more of your limit.

Why might emoji affect my count more than expected?

Because of how text is encoded, many emoji and some characters count as more than one character. An emoji-heavy post reaches the limit faster than its visible length suggests, which the counter reflects.

What if my thought is too long for one post?

Break it into a connected series of posts, each within the limit and building on the last. This is the accepted way to share a longer thought while respecting the character limit.
