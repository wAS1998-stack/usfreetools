What is URL Slug Generator?

A URL slug — the readable part of a web address after the domain — should be clean, lowercase, and keyword-rich, with words separated by hyphens. This generator turns any title or phrase into an SEO-friendly slug, stripping special characters, lowercasing, and joining words correctly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a good slug?

Short, lowercase, descriptive, keyword-relevant, hyphen-separated; avoid stop words and dates.

Hyphens or underscores?

Hyphens — Google treats them as word separators, while underscores join words.

Should I include the date?

Usually no — a dateless slug stays evergreen and lets you update content freely.

Should I use hyphens or underscores in URLs?

Use hyphens. Google reads hyphens as spaces between words but treats underscores as joining them, so 'red-shoes' is parsed correctly while 'red_shoes' is not.

Should slugs include dates?

Usually not. A date in the URL makes content look stale and complicates updates. Keep slugs short, descriptive, and evergreen.

Common Questions About SEO

A frequent question is whether meta tags still matter. They do, though not as a magic ranking lever — a strong title and description heavily influence whether people click your result, and click-through behavior feeds into performance over time. Writing unique, intent-matched tags within the display limits is basic craftsmanship that pays off, while duplicated or truncated tags quietly cost you clicks.

People also ask about keyword density. Modern search engines prioritize genuine relevance over repetition, so chasing a specific density figure is counterproductive; stuffing reads as spam and can be penalized. The useful role of a density check today is defensive — confirming your topic is present while catching accidental over-optimization. Write naturally for people first, and let the keywords follow the substance.

Finally, a common question is how length affects ranking. Word count is a correlation, not a cause: longer pages often rank because they cover a topic thoroughly and earn links, not because length itself is rewarded. The right approach is to answer the query completely and then stop — depth where it genuinely helps, never padding for its own sake.

Tips for the best results

Write unique, intent-matched meta tags within display limits, treat keyword density as a guardrail rather than a target, and size content to fully answer the query rather than to hit a number.

Expert Tips

  • Keep slugs short, lowercase, and keyword-relevant.
  • Use hyphens, not underscores — Google treats hyphens as word separators.
  • Avoid dates so URLs stay evergreen and updatable.
  • Drop stop words like 'a' and 'the' for cleaner slugs.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using underscores, which Google reads as joining words.
  • Stuffing long slugs with every keyword.
  • Embedding dates that make content look stale.
  • Leaving special characters that break or clutter URLs.

A clean slug helps both search engines and humans understand a page before they even visit it. The hyphen-versus-underscore distinction is real and often overlooked: Google reads hyphens as spaces between words but underscores as glue, so 'best-running-shoes' is parsed correctly while 'best_running_shoes' is not. Keep slugs short, evergreen, and descriptive.
