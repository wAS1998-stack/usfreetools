Frequently Asked Questions

Does it alert me when time runs out?

Yes — when the countdown reaches zero, the display changes to signal that time is up.

Can I pause and resume?

Yes. Pausing freezes the remaining time, and resuming continues the countdown from exactly where it stopped.

What is the maximum time I can set?

You can set up to 99 hours, plus minutes and seconds, which covers virtually any everyday use.

Does it keep counting if I switch tabs?

The countdown continues running while you are on other tabs, so you will not lose time by checking something else.

Does the countdown work for long durations?

Yes. You can set up to 99 hours plus minutes and seconds, so it handles everything from a 30-second plank to a multi-hour event. The display automatically shows hours only when your countdown is an hour or longer, keeping it easy to read.

Is it good for the Pomodoro technique?

It is ideal for it. Set 25 minutes for a focus block, start the countdown, and work until it reaches zero, then set a 5-minute break. Repeating this cycle is the core of the Pomodoro technique, a popular method for maintaining concentration and avoiding burnout during long work sessions.

Why Countdown Timers Boost Focus and Productivity

Countdown timers do something subtle but powerful: they make time visible. When a deadline is abstract, it is easy to lose track of it, but a ticking countdown creates a gentle, constant awareness that helps maintain focus and urgency. This is the principle behind popular productivity methods like the Pomodoro technique, which breaks work into timed intervals separated by short breaks.

The psychology is well studied. A visible time limit reduces procrastination by turning an open-ended task into a defined sprint, and the approaching zero point provides a natural motivation to stay on track. Countdown timers are equally valuable in the kitchen, the gym, and the classroom, anywhere a fixed duration needs to be observed without constant clock-watching.
