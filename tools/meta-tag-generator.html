Meta tags are small but important — they shape how your page appears in search results and when shared on social media. Writing them manually is fiddly and easy to get subtly wrong, with a missing attribute or a typo that breaks the tag silently. This generator produces correct tags every time, so you can focus on the content of your title and description rather than the syntax.

The Meta Tags That Matter Most

A few meta tags do most of the work. The title tag is the single most important — it is the clickable headline in search results and should be concise and descriptive, ideally under about 60 characters so it does not get cut off. The meta description is the summary text beneath the title in search results; while it is not a direct ranking factor, a compelling description improves how many people click, and it works best at around 150 to 160 characters.

Beyond those, the viewport tag tells mobile browsers how to scale your page, which is essential for it to display correctly on phones. The charset tag declares the character encoding so text renders properly. There is also the meta keywords tag, though it is worth knowing that major search engines no longer use it for ranking — it is largely a relic. This generator produces the tags that genuinely matter, formatted correctly.

Getting Your Title and Description Right

The tags are only as good as what you put in them, so the real work is writing a strong title and description. For the title, lead with the most important, specific words, since search engines and readers both weigh the beginning most heavily, and keep it within the display limit so nothing gets truncated. A good title tells someone exactly what they will find and gives them a reason to click, all in a few words.

For the description, treat it as a small advertisement for your page. Write it for the specific person searching, promise exactly what your page delivers, include your main keyword naturally (search engines bold matching words, which helps your result stand out), and stay within about 160 characters. Each page should have its own unique title and description rather than reusing the same ones, because duplicates waste the chance to tailor your pitch and can confuse search engines. Get these right and your meta tags do real work bringing visitors to your pages.

Quick Tips

  • Keep titles under about 60 characters and descriptions around 150 to 160 so neither gets cut off.
  • Give every page a unique title and description rather than reusing the same text.
  • Lead with your most important words, since the beginning carries the most weight.
  • Do not worry about the keywords tag for ranking — major search engines no longer use it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are meta tags?

They are snippets of HTML in a page's head section that describe the page to browsers, search engines, and social platforms. The most important are the title and description, which shape how your page appears in search results.

How long should my title and description be?

Keep the title under about 60 characters and the description around 150 to 160, so neither is truncated in search results. Front-load the most important words in case of any cutoff.

Does the meta keywords tag still matter?

Not for ranking. Major search engines stopped using the keywords tag long ago because it was abused. Focus your effort on a strong title and description instead.

Should every page have different meta tags?

Yes. Each page should have a unique title and description tailored to its content. Duplicate tags waste the opportunity to pitch each page and can confuse search engines.
