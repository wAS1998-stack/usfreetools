What This Tool Does

This tool generates placeholder text — “lorem ipsum” — for use in designs, mockups, and layouts before the real copy is ready. You choose how many paragraphs, sentences, or words you need, and it produces filler text instantly. Designers, developers, and writers use it to see how a layout looks with text in place without waiting on final content.

The point of placeholder text is that it looks like natural language without being readable, so it fills space convincingly while nobody is tempted to actually read it. That keeps attention on the design — the spacing, the typography, the flow — rather than on the words, which is exactly what you want when you are evaluating a layout.

Where Lorem Ipsum Comes From

Lorem ipsum is not random gibberish. It is scrambled, altered Latin derived from a work by the Roman writer Cicero, written more than two thousand years ago. The familiar passage has been used as placeholder text in printing and typesetting since at least the 1500s, when a printer is said to have jumbled the Latin to make a type specimen. It survived into digital design because it does its job so well.

That job is to mimic the rhythm and texture of real language — the distribution of word lengths and the cadence of sentences — without carrying meaning that would distract. Because most readers cannot parse the Latin, their eyes treat it as pure texture, which is precisely why it remains the standard placeholder after five centuries.

When to Use Placeholder Text (and When Not To)

Placeholder text shines in the early and middle stages of design, when you are working out structure, spacing, and hierarchy and the real copy does not exist yet. It lets you build and review layouts in parallel with the writing, rather than waiting for one before starting the other. For wireframes, templates, and design mockups, it is invaluable.

There is a real risk worth naming, though: placeholder text has a habit of escaping into finished work. Lorem ipsum has appeared on live websites, in printed materials, and even in published apps because someone forgot to swap it for real copy. Treat it as a temporary stand-in, mark it clearly if you can, and do a final pass before anything ships. Real copy also reveals layout problems that filler hides — text that is too long, headings that do not fit — so swap it in as early as the real words allow.

Quick Tips

  • Generate roughly the amount of text you expect the real copy to be, so the layout reflects reality.
  • Do a final check before publishing — placeholder text has a notorious habit of slipping into live work.
  • Swap in real copy as early as possible, since it exposes spacing issues that filler text masks.
  • For client mockups, consider labelling the placeholder so nobody mistakes it for finished content.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is lorem ipsum?

It is standard placeholder text used in design and publishing to fill space before real content is ready. It is scrambled, altered Latin that looks like natural language but carries no readable meaning, so it does not distract from the layout.

Why use placeholder text instead of real words?

Because real words pull attention to their meaning, while the goal at the layout stage is to evaluate spacing, typography, and flow. Lorem ipsum fills space convincingly without inviting anyone to read it.

Is lorem ipsum actually Latin?

It is derived from a Latin text by Cicero but has been scrambled and altered, so it is not coherent Latin and cannot be translated meaningfully. That scrambling is intentional — it keeps the text unreadable.

Can I generate a specific amount?

Yes. You can choose the number of paragraphs, sentences, or words you need, so the placeholder matches the expected length of your real content.
