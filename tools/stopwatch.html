⏱️ Online Stopwatch

A precise online stopwatch with lap timing. Start, stop, and record laps right in your browser — no app to install.

What This Tool Does

This tool is a stopwatch that counts time upward from zero, measuring how long something takes. Start it, stop it when you are done, and it shows the elapsed time — often with the ability to record laps along the way. It is the simple, accurate way to time anything, from a workout interval to a task you want to measure, right in your browser.

Where a countdown runs toward a set end, a stopwatch is open-ended — it measures duration as it happens, without needing to know in advance how long that will be. That makes it the right tool whenever you want to find out how long something takes rather than limit it to a set time, which is a genuinely common need.

Timing Up Versus Counting Down

The key difference from a countdown timer is direction and purpose. A stopwatch starts at zero and counts up, measuring elapsed time, so you use it when the duration is unknown and you want to record it. A countdown starts at a set value and runs to zero, so you use it to limit something to a fixed time. Choosing the right one comes down to a simple question: are you measuring how long something takes, or limiting it to a set length?

The lap function, where available, adds another dimension. It lets you mark intervals while the clock keeps running — recording the time of each lap without stopping the overall count. This is invaluable for anything with repeated segments, like timing each lap of a run or each round of an activity, because it captures the individual splits and the total together. Without laps you would have to stop and restart, losing the cumulative time; with them you get both at once.

Where a Stopwatch Earns Its Keep

Fitness and sport are the classic uses. Timing a run, a swim, a set of exercises, or rest intervals between them all call for measuring elapsed time, and the lap feature is perfect for recording each repetition while tracking the total. Athletes and anyone working out use a stopwatch to measure performance, pace efforts, and time intervals precisely — and seeing the exact duration helps with setting goals and tracking improvement over time.

Beyond exercise, a stopwatch is useful anywhere you want to know how long something takes. Timing how long a task actually takes helps with planning and estimating future work. Cooking steps, games, presentations rehearsals, and experiments all sometimes need measuring. People use stopwatches to build awareness of where their time goes, to challenge themselves to beat a previous time, or simply to answer the practical question “how long did that take?” Because it counts up open-endedly, it adapts to whatever you are timing without you needing to guess the length in advance.

Quick Tips

  • Use a stopwatch to measure how long something takes; use a countdown to limit it to a set time.
  • The lap function records intervals while the overall clock keeps running.
  • Timing how long tasks really take helps you plan and estimate future work more accurately.
  • For repeated segments like running laps, record each as a lap to capture splits and total together.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is a stopwatch different from a timer?

A stopwatch counts up from zero to measure how long something takes, while a countdown timer counts down from a set time to limit something. Use a stopwatch when the duration is unknown and you want to record it.

What does the lap function do?

It records the time of an interval while the overall clock keeps running, letting you capture individual splits and the cumulative total together — ideal for repeated segments like running laps or activity rounds.

When should I use a stopwatch?

Whenever you want to measure elapsed time: timing workouts and intervals, finding out how long a task takes, pacing efforts, or challenging yourself to beat a previous time.

Does it need to stay open?

The stopwatch runs in your browser, so keeping the page open lets it keep timing. It is designed to run while you measure whatever you are timing.
