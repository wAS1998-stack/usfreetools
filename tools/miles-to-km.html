🛣️ Miles to Kilometers

Convert miles to kilometers instantly for travel, running, and navigation.

What This Converter Does

This tool converts miles to kilometers instantly and accurately. Enter a distance in miles and it returns the precise equivalent in kilometers. It is the fastest way to translate the miles still used in the United States and the United Kingdom into the kilometers that are standard across most of the world.

This conversion comes up whenever distances cross between the two systems: planning a road trip abroad, reading a race distance, understanding a car's fuel economy or a speed limit in another country, or following directions written in the unit you do not normally use. The converter gives you an exact figure so you always know how far you are really going.

The Exact Conversion, and a Shortcut

One mile equals about 1.609 kilometers, so to convert miles to kilometers you multiply by 1.609. Ten miles is about 16.09 kilometers; a 26.2-mile marathon is roughly 42.2 kilometers. The converter uses the full-precision factor, which keeps you accurate over longer distances where small errors would otherwise add up.

For a quick mental estimate, multiply the miles by 1.6, or add about 60 percent. Fifty miles becomes roughly 80 kilometers that way. A couple of anchors help too: a marathon is about 42 kilometers, and 100 kilometers is roughly 62 miles. These rules of thumb are fine for rough planning, while the converter is there whenever you need the precise distance.

Where Miles-to-Kilometers Comes Up

Travel is the biggest driver. Drivers from the US or UK heading to a country that uses kilometers need to convert distances on road signs, map directions, and speed limits to judge how far and how fast they are going. A speed limit of 100 on a sign means 100 kilometers per hour, about 62 miles per hour — misreading it as miles could mean driving dangerously slowly or breaking the limit. Converting accurately keeps you safe and legal.

Running and fitness are another common area. Race distances, training plans, and fitness apps move between miles and kilometers depending on where they were created, so runners frequently convert to understand their distances and pace. Knowing that a 10K is about 6.2 miles, or that a half marathon is roughly 21 kilometers, helps you plan and compare efforts. Car fuel economy and odometer readings add further everyday reasons to convert between the two units.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using a flat 1.6 for precise needs instead of 1.609 — fine for estimates, but the gap grows over long distances.
  • Confusing miles per hour with kilometers per hour when reading a speed limit abroad — they are very different speeds.
  • Reversing the operation; multiply by 1.609 going miles to km, divide going km to miles.
  • Forgetting that a nautical mile is different again, used in sea and air travel, and not the same as a standard mile.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many kilometers are in a mile?

One mile equals about 1.609 kilometers. To convert, multiply your miles by 1.609. This converter uses the full-precision factor for an exact result.

What is a quick way to estimate miles to km?

Multiply by 1.6, or add about 60 percent. Fifty miles becomes roughly 80 kilometers. It is good enough for rough planning, though not for precise distance work.

How do I convert kilometers back to miles?

Divide the kilometers by 1.609, or multiply by about 0.62. One hundred kilometers is about 62 miles. The converter handles both directions precisely.

How long is a marathon in kilometers?

A marathon is 26.2 miles, which is about 42.2 kilometers. A half marathon is roughly 21 kilometers, and a 10K race is about 6.2 miles.
