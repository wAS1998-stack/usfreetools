🎓 Grade Calculator

Calculate your overall weighted grade from individual scores and their weights, complete with a letter grade. Add as many assignments as you need.

What This Calculator Does

This calculator works out your grade from your scores, including weighted grades where different assignments count for different proportions of the total. Enter your scores and their weights, and it calculates your overall grade. It takes the complexity out of figuring out where you stand in a course, especially when exams, assignments, and projects each count for a different share of the final mark.

Working out a weighted grade by hand is genuinely error-prone, because you cannot just average the scores — each must be multiplied by its weight and combined correctly. This calculator handles that arithmetic, so students can accurately track their standing, see what they need on remaining work, and understand how each component affects their final grade.

How Weighted Grades Work

Many courses do not weight all work equally — a final exam might count for 40% of your grade, homework for 20%, a project for 25%, and participation for 15%. To find your overall grade, you cannot simply average your scores; you must multiply each score by its weight, then add those weighted contributions together. A high score on a heavily weighted exam moves your grade far more than the same score on lightly weighted homework, which is the whole point of weighting.

This is where mistakes happen by hand: it is easy to forget a weight, misapply one, or just average everything and get a misleading answer. The calculator does it correctly, taking each score and its weight and combining them into the accurate overall grade. Understanding the principle helps you use the result well — it shows you that improving your score on a high-weight component has a bigger payoff, and that a poor score on a small-weight piece hurts less than it might feel. Seeing the weighted math laid out makes the structure of your grade clear.

Using a Grade Calculator Well

The most practical use is knowing where you stand and planning ahead. By entering the scores you have so far with their weights, you see your current grade accurately, rather than guessing from a rough average that ignores weighting. This is reassuring when you are doing well and a useful early warning when you are not. Crucially, it lets you plan: by trying different scores for upcoming assignments or exams, you can see what you would need on remaining work to reach a target grade, which turns a vague hope into a concrete goal.

This kind of planning is genuinely empowering for students. Knowing that you need, say, a certain score on the final to keep your desired grade lets you direct your effort where it matters most, and knowing that a particular assignment carries little weight helps you prioritize sensibly when time is short. It also removes anxiety born of uncertainty — seeing the actual numbers is usually clearer and calmer than worrying about an unknown standing. Used throughout a course rather than just at the end, a grade calculator helps you stay informed, set realistic targets, and make smart decisions about where to focus, all based on an accurate picture of how your grade is actually built.

Quick Tips

  • Try different scores for upcoming work to see what you need to reach a target grade.
  • Check your standing throughout the course, not just at the end, to plan your effort.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is a weighted grade calculated?

Each score is multiplied by its weight (the share of the grade it represents), and the weighted results are added together. You cannot simply average the scores, because they count for different proportions of the total.

Why can't I just average my scores?

Because assignments are often weighted differently — an exam worth 40% counts far more than homework worth 10%. A plain average treats them equally, giving a misleading result. Weighting reflects how the course actually scores you.

How do I find what I need on a final exam?

Enter your current scores and weights, then try different scores for the final to see which reaches your target grade. This turns the question “what do I need?” into a concrete number to aim for.

Is my information private?

Yes. All calculation happens in your browser, so nothing you enter is uploaded or stored. You can work out your grades privately.
