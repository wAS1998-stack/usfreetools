⏎ Remove Line Breaks

Remove unwanted line breaks from text copied from PDFs and documents, joining it into clean paragraphs.

What This Tool Does

This tool strips unwanted line breaks out of text, turning a block broken across many lines into clean, flowing paragraphs. Paste text that has awkward breaks in the middle of sentences — the kind you get when copying from a PDF, an email, or a code editor — and it joins it back into continuous prose. It saves you from the tedious work of deleting line breaks one by one.

This is one of those small problems that is genuinely annoying when you hit it. Text copied from certain sources arrives with a hard line break at the end of every visual line, so when you paste it elsewhere it looks broken and reads badly. This tool fixes that in one step, giving you text you can actually use.

Where Stray Line Breaks Come From

The usual culprit is copying text from a source that wraps lines with hard breaks rather than letting them flow. PDFs are the worst offenders — each visible line often ends with an actual line break, so a single paragraph becomes a dozen broken lines when you paste it. Plain-text emails, some code comments, and text from older documents have the same problem. The text looks fine in its original home but falls apart when moved.

The fix is to remove those hard breaks while being careful to preserve the breaks you actually want — the ones between real paragraphs. A good line-break remover distinguishes between a break in the middle of a sentence (unwanted) and a break between paragraphs (wanted), so you end up with clean, properly separated paragraphs rather than one giant wall of text. This tool handles that distinction so your cleaned text stays readable.

Cleaning Text for Real Work

This kind of cleanup matters whenever you are moving text between systems. Researchers and students copying quotes from PDF sources need clean prose to paste into their documents. Writers and editors pulling text from various sources need it to flow properly before they can work with it. Anyone preparing content for a website, a newsletter, or a document benefits from text that is free of the random breaks that make formatting look broken.

Beyond line breaks, messy pasted text often carries other invisible problems — extra spaces, inconsistent spacing, or stray characters. The goal of cleaning is always the same: text that flows naturally and formats predictably wherever you put it. Removing line breaks is usually the biggest single improvement, turning unusable broken text into something you can immediately work with, which is why it is such a frequently needed little tool.

Quick Tips

  • Use this whenever text copied from a PDF or email arrives broken across many short lines.
  • Paragraph breaks you want to keep are preserved — only the unwanted mid-sentence breaks are removed.
  • Clean the text before formatting it elsewhere, so your final layout is not disrupted by hidden breaks.
  • If text also has extra spaces, a whitespace cleanup is a good follow-up step.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does text from PDFs have so many line breaks?

Many PDFs end each visible line with a hard line break rather than letting text flow. When you copy and paste it, those breaks come along, turning a paragraph into many broken lines. This tool removes them.

Will it remove the breaks between my paragraphs too?

No. The tool distinguishes unwanted mid-sentence breaks from the breaks between real paragraphs, preserving your paragraph structure while joining the broken lines within each one.

Is my text uploaded anywhere?

No. All processing happens in your browser, so your text never leaves your device. You can clean private or sensitive text safely.

What else causes broken-looking pasted text?

Besides line breaks, pasted text can carry extra spaces or stray characters. Removing line breaks usually fixes the biggest problem; a whitespace cleanup handles the rest if needed.
