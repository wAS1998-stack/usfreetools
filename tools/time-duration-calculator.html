⏳ Time Duration Calculator

Calculate the duration in hours and minutes between two times — perfect for timesheets and scheduling.

What This Calculator Does

This calculator finds the duration between two times — how many hours and minutes lie between a start time and an end time. Enter the two times and it works out the elapsed duration, handling the awkward arithmetic of time, including spans that cross midnight. It is the quick, reliable way to calculate how long something lasted or will last, without counting on a clock by hand.

Working out the time between two clock times is more error-prone than it seems, because time is not decimal — there are 60 minutes in an hour, not 100, so you cannot just subtract the numbers. This calculator handles that correctly, giving you an accurate duration for timesheets, schedules, planning, and any situation where you need to know exactly how much time elapsed.

Why Time Arithmetic Is Tricky

The difficulty with calculating durations comes from time's base-60 structure. Minutes go from 0 to 59 before rolling over to the next hour, so subtracting times means borrowing across that boundary rather than the familiar base-10 borrowing — the gap from 1:45 to 2:30 is 45 minutes, not the 15 you might get by naively subtracting the minute figures. This trips people up constantly, especially when the minutes of the end time are fewer than those of the start time.

Crossing midnight adds another layer. If something starts in the evening and ends in the early morning, the end time's number is smaller than the start's, and a naive subtraction gives a nonsensical negative result. The correct calculation accounts for the day boundary, recognizing that the duration wraps around midnight. These two complications — base-60 minutes and the midnight wrap — are exactly the kind of thing that is easy to get wrong by hand and easy for a calculator to get right every time, which is why a tool is genuinely useful here.

Where Duration Calculation Helps

Tracking work hours is one of the most common uses. Calculating how long a shift lasted, totaling hours for a timesheet, or working out billable time all require finding the duration between a start and end time accurately — and since this often affects pay, getting it right matters. The base-60 arithmetic and shifts that cross midnight (like overnight work) are precisely where manual calculations go wrong, so a calculator that handles them reliably is valuable for anyone logging hours, whether for employment, freelancing, or their own records.

Duration calculation helps with planning and everyday timing too. Working out how long an event, journey, activity, or appointment will last; figuring out how much time you have between two commitments; planning schedules where durations need to add up; or simply answering “how long was that?” all rely on the same arithmetic. It is useful for cooking and activities timed by the clock, for understanding how long you spent on something, and for coordinating schedules. Because the calculator handles the tricky parts — the minute boundaries and the midnight crossing — automatically, it turns a fiddly mental calculation into an instant, dependable answer.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the duration between two times calculated?

The calculator finds the elapsed time between a start and end time, correctly handling the base-60 nature of minutes and spans that cross midnight, giving an accurate hours-and-minutes result.

Why can't I just subtract the times?

Because time is base-60, not base-10 — there are 60 minutes in an hour. Naively subtracting the numbers gives wrong answers, especially when the end minutes are fewer than the start minutes, which requires borrowing across the hour.

Does it handle times that cross midnight?

Yes. For a span that starts in the evening and ends after midnight, the calculator accounts for the day boundary, avoiding the negative result a naive subtraction would produce.
