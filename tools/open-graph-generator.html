📱 Open Graph Generator

Generate Open Graph meta tags so your pages display perfect previews when shared on social media.

What This Tool Does

This tool generates Open Graph meta tags — the special HTML tags that control how your page looks when shared on social media. Fill in details like the title, description, and image, and it produces the tags you paste into your page's head section. With them in place, a link to your page shows a rich preview card with your chosen title, description, and image instead of a bare, unappealing link.

When someone shares your page on social platforms, those platforms look for Open Graph tags to build the preview. Without them, the preview is unpredictable and often unattractive — a missing image, the wrong text, a stripped-down link. Setting these tags puts you in control of that first impression, which strongly affects whether people click. This generator builds the correct tags for you.

What Open Graph Tags Control

Open Graph is a standard, originally from Facebook and now used across most social platforms, for describing a web page's content. The key tags are the title (the headline of the preview), the description (the summary text beneath it), the image (the picture shown in the card), the URL (the canonical link), and the type (such as website or article). Together these define the preview card that appears when your link is shared.

The image tag deserves special attention, because the image is what catches the eye in a feed and does the most to attract clicks. Platforms expect images of a certain size and aspect ratio to display well, and an image that is too small or the wrong shape gets cropped awkwardly or not shown at all. A well-chosen, correctly sized image paired with a compelling title is what turns a shared link into something people actually click. The generator produces the tags; choosing a strong title, clear description, and good image is what makes them effective.

Why Open Graph Tags Matter

Social sharing is a major way people discover content, and the preview card is the entire first impression. A link shared with no Open Graph tags might appear as plain text or with a random, unhelpful image scraped from the page — uninviting, and easy to scroll past. The same link with proper tags appears as a polished card with a clear headline, an enticing description, and an eye-catching image. The difference in how many people click can be substantial, which makes these tags genuinely worth setting for any page you want shared.

Beyond individual clicks, consistent, well-crafted previews build trust and recognition. When your shared links reliably look professional, people associate that quality with your brand or site. It also gives you control over the message: you decide exactly what title and description represent the page socially, rather than leaving it to chance. A few practical habits help — give each page its own tags rather than reusing one set, choose an image that works at preview size, and keep the title and description compelling but accurate. Testing how a link previews before relying on it is wise too, since fixing it after wide sharing is harder. Done well, Open Graph tags turn every share into effective, attractive promotion.

Quick Tips

  • The image is what catches the eye — choose a strong one sized correctly for social previews.
  • Give each page its own Open Graph tags rather than reusing a single set across the site.
  • Write a compelling but accurate title and description, since these drive whether people click.
  • Test how your link previews before relying on it, as fixing it after wide sharing is harder.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Open Graph tags?

They are HTML meta tags that control how your page appears when shared on social media — defining the title, description, and image of the preview card. Without them, social previews are unpredictable and often unattractive.

Why is the Open Graph image important?

The image is what catches the eye in a social feed and does the most to attract clicks. It needs to be a good size and aspect ratio for previews, or platforms may crop it awkwardly or not show it.

Do all social platforms use Open Graph?

Most do. Open Graph originated with Facebook but is now widely supported across social platforms for building link previews, making it the standard worth implementing for social sharing.

Should each page have different tags?

Yes. Each page should have its own title, description, and image tags reflecting its content, so every shared link previews accurately rather than showing the same generic card.
